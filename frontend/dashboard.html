The AI Engine never conditions difficulty or feedback on cohort membership; this panel exists purely to help instructors detect and investigate outcome disparities after the fact (see Ethics & Privacy Layer). A cohort is only flagged for review when its deviation is both practically large (≥15 points) and statistically significant (Welch's t-test, p < 0.05) against the rest of the population.

AI Technique Comparison (EMA vs. Bayesian Knowledge Tracing)

EMA drives the real difficulty recommendation shown to learners. BKT is computed in parallel on the same attempts purely to compare estimator behaviour, per R1's "examine effectiveness of various AI techniques."
